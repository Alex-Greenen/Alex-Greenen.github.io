My MMath dissertation explored the topic of incompressible quantum fluids, which express ideal fluid dynamics in terms of two wave functions evolved via Schrodinger’s equation. The report discusses the mathematical underpinnings of these incompressible quantum fluids by looking into Clebsch variables and how they allow for an insightful representation of vortical structures in fluids. Notably, we investigate a map taking vortex lines in the fluids to points on a sphere in an organised fashion. I further programmed an algorithm to efficiently evolve these fluids numerically using the VTK vizualisaition library. The algorithm allows for the consideration of various numerical methods and is tested by using it to simulate jets and vortex rings. The report then presents the outcome of the testing.

Over summer 2018, I researched the analytical form of force-free magnetic flux tubes on the solar corona. This project was funded by the Royal Astronomical Society (RAS) and St Chad's College.

Over summer 2019, I researched the bridge between Anti de-Sitter Space and Conformal Field Theories using holograpy. I notably looked into the recovery rates of different tilings of AdS-Space using perfect tensors (i.e. HaPPY Code).

In my free time, I learned C++ and coded a graphics rendering programme that includes different types of material effects and ray tracing (see image).

On my personal time, I programmed from scratch in Swift (IOS) a cloth simulation taking in account elasticity, gravity and self-interaction.

This cloud-based IOS app was created over 24 hours during the 2019 Durham Hackathon. It is a posting app for local services which comes with login security, post creation and viewing, and live messaging. It is programed in Swift with PHP and MySQL backend connected to a locally-hosted server.
